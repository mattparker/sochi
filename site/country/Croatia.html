Croatian alpine skier Janica Kostelic dominated the 2002 Olympic Winter Games in Salt Lake City by winning three gold medals [combined, slalom and giant slalom] and a silver [super G]. She became the first alpine skier to win four medals at a single winter Olympic competition and also equalled the Olympic record of Austria's Anton Sailer [1956] and Frenchman Jean-Claude Killy [1968], who both won three alpine skiing gold medals at a single Olympic Winter Games.
